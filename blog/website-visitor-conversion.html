Your Website Had 200 Visitors This Week. How Many Did You Speak To?

5 min read Apr 5, 2026 Coremagna

200 visitors. Average website conversion rate: 2.35% (WordStream). That's 4 leads. 196 people visited your website, looked around, and left without a word. Most of them you'll never see again. A few of them were ready to buy.

The Invisible Visitor Problem

98% of website visitors leave without taking any action (Marketo). They don't fill out a form, don't call, don't email. They were anonymous when they arrived and they're anonymous when they leave. Your analytics shows they were there. Your CRM has no idea they existed.

The top 25% of websites convert at 5.31% or higher. The gap between 2.35% and 5.31% on 200 visitors is the difference between 4 leads and 10. On 1,000 visitors, that's 23 additional leads per week — from the same traffic you're already paying to generate.

What Visitors Actually Want at 11pm

When someone lands on your website at 11pm, they have a question. Maybe it's about pricing. Maybe they want to know if you work with businesses their size. Maybe they just need to know if you're available before they invest more time researching. They don't want to fill out a form and wait two days for a reply. They want an answer now.

A static website can't give them that. It can only show them information and hope they're patient enough to submit a form. Most aren't — they close the tab and try the next result.

What a Chatbot Changes

An AI chatbot on your website answers questions immediately, at any hour. It handles the standard queries — pricing, availability, service scope, next steps. When the visitor is ready to move forward, it captures their name and email and offers to book a call directly in your calendar. By the time you open your laptop in the morning, the conversation has already happened and the lead is in your CRM.

The data supports this. AI chatbots resolve 90% of queries without human involvement (IBM). Businesses deploying them see an average 20% increase in conversion rates — not from more traffic, just from the same visitors being met with an immediate response instead of a form.
