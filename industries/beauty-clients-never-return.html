The Gap They Fall Into

Great Service Isn’t Enough The Gap After They Leave Is Where You Lose Them

They walked out thrilled. You never heard from them again. Here’s why — and why it’s not your fault.

No Follow-Up

They walked out thrilled. You never messaged them. Six weeks later they’ve forgotten your name — but not the salon their friend just recommended on Instagram.

No Rebooking Reminder

The average gap between salon visits is 10–11 weeks. Without a nudge at week 4–6, clients drift. Only 29% of new clients rebook on their own.

Source: Phorest, 2025

No Loyalty Recognition

She’s been coming for two years. Twenty-four visits. She has no idea you appreciate her — because you’ve never told her. Meanwhile the new salon down the road just offered her a loyalty card.

No Upselling System

She’d love a treatment add-on. She’d buy that product you recommended. But nobody ever followed up with a personalised suggestion — because there’s no system to do it.
