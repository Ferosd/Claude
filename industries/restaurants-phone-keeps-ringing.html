The Problem

Every Ring During Service Is a Lose-Lose Decision

The phone rings. Somebody has to answer it. Nobody can. This plays out 5–10 times every service — and every time it does, a guest books elsewhere.

The Impossible Choice

The phone rings during service. Your server stops mid-order to answer it. The guest at table 6 waits. The caller gets a rushed, distracted response. Both experiences suffer. You can't win — but you also can't not answer.

91% expect hold times under 3 minutes HungerRush, 2024

The After-Hours Black Hole

It's 10:30 PM. Someone wants to book a birthday dinner for 12 next Saturday. They call. Nobody answers. They don't leave a voicemail — 80% never do. By Monday morning they've already booked your competitor.

80% hang up on voicemail Ambs Call Center, 2025

The Staffing Spiral

You can't hire someone just to answer phones — not with 38% staff turnover. Your host is already stretched. Your manager is firefighting. The phone is everyone's job, which means it's nobody's job.
