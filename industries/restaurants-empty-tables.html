The Problem

It’s Not Just the Table. It’s Everything Behind It.

You’re running on 3–5% net margins. Every no-show isn’t just a missed cover — it’s wasted food, idle staff, and a walk-in you turned away for a guest who never walked through the door.

The Direct Hit: Lost Revenue

A 4-top no-show at £75 per head is £300 gone instantly. That’s not a rounding error — it happens 2–4 times per night in busy restaurants. Over a month, that’s £18,000–£36,000 in covers that never materialised.

£300

per 4-top no-show at £75/person. Source: Hostie AI, 2025

The Hidden Cost: Wasted Food & Labour

Your kitchen prepped 6 covers that nobody ate. Staff stood around an empty section for two hours. You already lose 6% of total sales to food waste — no-shows push that figure higher every weekend.

6%

of restaurant sales lost through food waste, much from no-shows. Source: Restroworks, 2026

The Worst Part: Turned-Away Walk-Ins

While those two tables sat empty, four walk-ins were told “Sorry, we’re fully booked tonight.” They went to the restaurant next door. You lost the no-show revenue and the walk-in revenue. Twice the loss from one failure to confirm.

×2 Loss
